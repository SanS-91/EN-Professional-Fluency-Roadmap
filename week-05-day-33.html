Managing Expectations

C1 speakers don't say "yes" and burn out, or "no" and burn bridges. They say "yes, if..." or "no, but..." — giving others the choice while protecting their own capacity. Hôm nay bạn sẽ học 5 patterns để chủ động thiết lập kỳ vọng rõ ràng.

Today's Philosophy

From "People Pleaser" to "Strategic Partner"

Nhiều người nghĩ "managing expectations" là thao túng hoặc ra lệnh. Thực tế, nó chỉ là làm cho người khác dễ làm việc với bạn. Khi bạn rõ ràng về capacity, timeline, và trade-offs, người khác sẽ tin tưởng bạn hơn.

Bad news early is a strategy. Bad news late is a crisis.

No register is "better" than another. Casual, neutral, and professional are all correct when they match the context. The lesson teaches appropriateness, not hierarchy.

Why most people fail at expectations
  • Nói "Yes" với mọi thứ vì sợ mất lòng → overpromise, underdeliver
  • Giấu tin xấu cho đến phút chót vì sợ bị đánh giá
  • Để người khác tự đoán "done" nghĩa là gì → dẫn đến thất vọng
  • Hỏi "Em phải làm gì?" thay vì đưa ra options
What C1 communicators do differently
  • Offer trade-offs: "I can do X, but Y will have to wait"
  • Flag early: "I want to flag that we might miss the Friday deadline"
  • Define "done" upfront: "By Friday, you'll have the draft, but not the final polish"
  • Ask for priorities: "Should I focus on X or Y first?"
💡 About connectors: Native speakers dùng nhiều cách để đưa ra điều kiện: if, as long as, assuming, so long as, provided. Đừng đóng khung vào 1 từ — học cách dùng linh hoạt tùy ngữ cảnh.

5 Managing Expectations Patterns

5 patterns để chủ động thiết lập kỳ vọng. Mỗi pattern có register progression và Life/Business examples. Click 🔊 để nghe.

1
The Capacity Check (Trade-offs)
Core idea: "I'm too busy to do this."
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
When you need to say yes with conditions
Đưa ra sự lựa chọn, không phải từ chối. Chuyển giao quyền quyết định một cách chuyên nghiệp.

Register Progression 🔊

🏠 Casual
"I can help with X, if that means we skip Y."
Bạn bè, gia đình
☕ Neutral
"I can take a look, as long as it doesn't clash with Friday's deadline."
Đồng nghiệp
💼 Professional
"I can take that on, assuming we push the Q3 review to next week."
Sếp, Client
📋 More Formal
"I'm happy to lead this, so long as we have the budget for it."
Senior leaders

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "I can help with the party invitations, if that means you handle the grocery run today."
🏠 Life "I can drive you to the airport, as long as you're ready by 6 AM."
💼 Business "I can take that on, assuming we push the Q3 review to next week."
💼 Business "I'm happy to lead this, so long as we have the budget for it."
⚠️ Common mistake:
"I can't do that, I have too much on my plate." (Nghe như phàn nàn, victim mindset)
"I can do that, provided we pause the other project." (Nghe như project manager, đưa ra lựa chọn)
Why native speakers dùng:
  • "If" / "as long as" / "assuming" / "so long as" = shifts burden of choice to them
  • Không nghe lazy hay incompetent
  • Bạn đang nói "yes" với công việc, nhưng đặt điều kiện
2
The Early Warning (Flag Early)
Core idea: "It's going to be late / fail."
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
When you see a risk early
Báo tin xấu sớm. Cho phép người khác điều chỉnh plan, thể hiện sự kiểm soát.

Register Progression 🔊

🏠 Casual
"Just a heads-up, I'm running a bit behind on..."
Bạn bè, gia đình
☕ Neutral
"I want to give you a quick heads-up that..."
Đồng nghiệp
💼 Professional
"I want to flag early that we might miss the Friday deadline due to..."
Sếp, Client
📋 More Formal
"I want to manage expectations upfront — the timeline is at risk because..."
Senior leaders

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "Just a heads-up, I'm running a bit behind on the reservations, so we might not get the window table."
🏠 Life "Just a heads-up, the flight might be delayed — I'll keep you posted."
💼 Business "I want to flag early that we might miss the Friday deadline due to the legal review. I'm pushing them, but I wanted you to know."
💼 Business "I want to manage expectations upfront — the timeline is at risk because the vendor is delayed."
⚠️ Common mistake:
(Im lặng cho đến thứ Sáu) "Sorry, it's not done." (Gây khủng hoảng, mất niềm tin)
(Nói vào thứ Tư) "I want to flag early..." (Cho phép sếp điều chỉnh plan, thể hiện sự kiểm soát)
Why native speakers dùng:
  • "Flag early" and "heads-up" frame the delay as a shared problem to solve
  • Không phải personal failure
  • Bad news early is a strategy; bad news late is a crisis
3
The Priority Clarifier
Core idea: "What do you want me to do first?"
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
When you can't do both
Hỏi thứ tự ưu tiên. Cho thấy bạn hiểu rõ capacity của mình, chủ động thay vì bị động.

Register Progression 🔊

🏠 Casual
"Which one first?" / "Should I tackle X or Y?"
Bạn bè, gia đình
☕ Neutral
"Just to check, should I focus on X or Y first?"
Đồng nghiệp
💼 Professional
"To make sure I'm focusing on the right things, should I prioritize X or Y?"
Sếp, Client
📋 More Formal
"Given our current bandwidth, which of these two initiatives takes precedence?"
Senior leaders

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "We only have one afternoon. Which one first — pack the car or clean the kitchen?"
🏠 Life "Should I tackle the grocery shopping or the laundry first?"
💼 Business "To make sure I'm focusing on the right things, should I prioritize the client deck or the internal budget report? I can get one done by 5 PM."
💼 Business "Just to check, should I focus on the Q3 review or the client proposal first?"
⚠️ Common mistake:
"Which one do you want?" (Nghe bị động, bắt sếp phải nghĩ hộ bạn)
"To make sure I'm focusing on the right things..." (Nghe chủ động, cho thấy bạn hiểu rõ capacity)
Why native speakers dùng:
  • "Which one first?" = short, casual, efficient
  • "Should I tackle X or Y?" = shows you're ready to act
  • "To make sure I'm focusing..." = professional framing
  • Forces them to choose, not you
4
The Scope Anchor (Define "Done")
Core idea: "Here is exactly what I will do (and won't do)."
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
When you need to set boundaries
Định nghĩa chữ "Xong" rõ ràng. Chặn đứng sự thất vọng trước khi nó xảy ra.

Register Progression 🔊

🏠 Casual
"I'll do X, but not Y." / "Just so you know, I'll only have time for the draft."
Bạn bè, gia đình
☕ Neutral
"Just to align, I'll send you the draft today, but the final polish will be tomorrow."
Đồng nghiệp
💼 Professional
"Just to align on expectations, by Friday I'll have the wireframes ready, but not the final UI."
Sếp, Client
📋 More Formal
"To set clear expectations, this phase covers X, but excludes Y."
Senior leaders

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "Just so you know, I'll only have time for the draft tonight. The final version can wait till tomorrow."
🏠 Life "I'll book the flights and hotel, but you'll need to handle the car rental and itinerary."
💼 Business "Just to align on expectations, by Friday I'll have the wireframes ready, but not the final UI. That will be next week."
💼 Business "To set clear expectations, this phase covers the research, but excludes the implementation."
⚠️ Common mistake:
"I'll work on the design." (Mơ hồ. Sếp tưởng là bản final, bạn mới làm bản nháp)
"Just to align on expectations, by Friday I'll have X, but not Y." (Chặn đứng sự thất vọng)
Why native speakers dùng:
  • "Just to align" = collaborative framing
  • "Just so you know" = casual heads-up
  • "To set clear expectations" = professional boundary
  • Prevents the "I thought you meant..." conversation later
5
Asking for Support (Removing Blockers)
Core idea: "I'm stuck and need you to do your job so I can do mine."
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
When you need help to move forward
Xin trợ giúp một cách chuyên nghiệp. Tập trung vào mục tiêu chung, không đổ lỗi cá nhân.

Register Progression 🔊

🏠 Casual
"I can't figure this out. Can you give me a hand?"
Bạn bè, gia đình
☕ Neutral
"I'm a bit stuck on the formatting. Could you take a quick look?"
Đồng nghiệp
💼 Professional
"I've hit a bottleneck with the client data. To keep us on track, I'll need your approval."
Sếp, Client
📋 More Formal
"To unblock the team and maintain the timeline, we require a decision on..."
Senior leaders

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "I've hit a wall with the tax forms. To get this done tonight, I'll need you to find the W-2s."
🏠 Life "I'm stuck on assembling this IKEA furniture. Can you give me a hand?"
💼 Business "I've hit a bottleneck with the client data. To keep us on track, I'll need your approval on the budget by tomorrow morning."
💼 Business "To unblock the team and maintain the timeline, we require a decision on the vendor selection by EOD."
⚠️ Common mistake:
"I can't finish because you haven't approved the budget." (Nghe như đổ lỗi, aggressive)
"To keep us on track, I'll need..." (Tập trung vào mục tiêu chung, không đổ lỗi cá nhân)
Why native speakers dùng:
  • "Hit a bottleneck" / "hit a wall" = neutral, not blaming
  • "To keep us on track" = shared goal framing
  • "I'll need" = clear, direct, professional
  • Focuses on the blocker, not the person

Integrated Dialogues

Xem cách 5 expectation patterns được dùng trong 2 dialogues hoàn chỉnh (10+ lượt). Click 🔊 để nghe.

🏠 Dialogue 1: The Family Reunion Overload

Context: Minh và em gái (Sarah) đang lên kế hoạch cho buổi họp mặt gia đình 50 người. Sarah liên tục giao thêm việc cho Minh.
Sarah:
"Minh, can you also design the photo booth backdrop and call the caterer to change the menu?"
Minh:
"I can call the caterer, as long as you don't mind me skipping the grocery run today. I only have two hands."
Sarah:
"But the photo booth is going to be so cute! Can't you just do it tonight?"
Minh:
"Just to align on expectations, if I do the backdrop tonight, I won't have time to confirm the hotel blocks for the cousins."
Sarah:
"Ugh, okay. The hotel blocks are more important."
Minh:
"To make sure I'm focusing on the right things, should I prioritize the caterer or the hotel blocks first thing tomorrow?"
Sarah:
"Hotel blocks. Definitely."
Minh:
"Got it. Just a heads-up, the caterer might charge a fee for the late menu change. I wanted to flag that early."
Sarah:
"Oh, good catch. Just ask them how much it is before we commit."
Minh:
"Will do. I've hit a bottleneck with the guest list, though. I need Mom to finalize her side of the family."
Sarah:
"I'll text her right now."
Minh:
"Perfect. Thanks!"

Analysis — Pattern Usage:

  • "As long as you don't mind me skipping" → Capacity Check (Trade-off)
  • "Just to align on expectations" → Scope Anchor (Defines "done")
  • "To make sure I'm focusing on the right things" → Priority Clarifier
  • "Just a heads-up" → Early Warning (Flag early)
  • "I've hit a bottleneck with" → Asking for Support (Removing blocker)
  • Register: Casual throughout (siblings)
  • 12 lượt hội thoại tự nhiên, áp dụng kỹ năng professional để không bị overworked

💼 Dialogue 2: The Friday Afternoon Dump

Context: 4:00 PM thứ Sáu. Manager (Lisa) giao cho Minh 2 task "khẩn cấp".
Lisa:
"Minh, I need the Q3 financial model updated, and also the slide deck for Monday's board meeting. Both are urgent."
Minh:
"I want to flag early that doing both by Monday morning is going to be tight. The model alone takes about 6 hours."
Lisa:
"I know it's a lot, but the board really needs both."
Minh:
"To make sure I'm focusing on the right things, should I prioritize the slide deck or the financial model first? I can get one 100% done, or both at 70% quality."
Lisa:
"Quality matters for the deck. The model can have some estimates."
Minh:
"Okay. Just to align on expectations, I'll have the final slide deck in your inbox by 8 PM tonight. For the model, I'll provide the high-level estimates, but not the granular breakdown."
Lisa:
"That works. Thanks for being flexible."
Minh:
"No problem. To keep us on track for next week, I'll need your sign-off on the deck by 9 AM tomorrow so I can print the handouts."
Lisa:
"I'll review it first thing in the morning."
Minh:
"Perfect. Have a good evening, Lisa."
Lisa:
"You too, Minh."

Analysis — Pattern Usage:

  • "I want to flag early that" → Early Warning (Proactive, not complaining)
  • "To make sure I'm focusing on the right things" → Priority Clarifier (Forces manager to choose)
  • "Just to align on expectations... but not..." → Scope Anchor (Defines "done")
  • "To keep us on track, I'll need..." → Asking for Support (Sets boundary for weekend/morning)
  • Register: Professional. Giữ được relationship mà không phải làm việc quá sức vô lý.
  • 11 lượt hội thoại, thấy register shift và problem-solving

🎵 Intonation Patterns — When & Why

"I want to flag early that..." ↘ Falling Context: Dùng khi báo tin xấu sớm. Falling tone = factual, calm, in control. Tránh: Rising (nghe như bạn đang hoảng loạn).
"To make sure I'm focusing on the right things..." ↗↘ Rise-fall Context: Dùng khi hỏi priority. Rise-fall = collaborative, helpful. Tránh: Flat (nghe robotic).
"Just to align on expectations..." ↘ Falling Context: Dùng khi định nghĩa scope. Falling tone = firm, clear boundary. Tránh: Up-talk (nghe như bạn đang xin phép).
"As long as you don't mind..." ↗ Rising Context: Dùng khi đưa ra trade-off nhẹ nhàng. Rising = polite, checking for agreement. Tránh: Falling (nghe như demand).
"To keep us on track, I'll need..." ↘ Falling Context: Dùng khi xin support. Falling tone = firm but respectful. Tránh: Too soft (nghe uncertain).

Practice Scenarios

Practice managing expectations in different contexts. Remember: no register is "better" — it's about appropriateness!

🟢 Casual - 3-4 min

Scenario 1: The Overloaded Weekend

Bạn bè rủ bạn đi phượt, nhưng bạn đang phải dọn nhà và làm việc vặt.

Requirements:

  • Dùng "I can help/do X, as long as..."
  • Dùng "Just a heads-up..."
  • Dùng "Which one first?"
  • Keep it light, not confrontational
🔵 Neutral - 3-4 min

Scenario 2: Colleague's "Quick Favor"

Đồng nghiệp nhờ bạn "review nhanh" một cái proposal 50 trang vào cuối ngày.

Requirements:

  • Dùng "Just to align on expectations, I can read the executive summary, but not the whole thing."
  • Dùng "I've hit a bottleneck..."
  • Define "done" rõ ràng
🟣 Professional - 4-5 min

Scenario 3: The Scope Creep Client

Client email đòi thêm 3 tính năng mới khi project sắp close.

Requirements:

  • Dùng "I want to flag early..."
  • Dùng "To keep us on track, I'll need..."
  • Offer trade-offs, not just "no"
  • Keep relationship intact
🟠 Challenge - 5 min

Scenario 4: The Double Boundary

Sáng: Từ chối nhận thêm việc nhà từ spouse (Casual). Chiều: Clarify priority với sếp khi bị giao 2 task urgent (Professional).

Requirements:

  • Shift register linh hoạt
  • Define "done" rõ ràng ở cả 2 context
  • Mỗi conversation có opening + elaboration + closing
  • Use different patterns for each
