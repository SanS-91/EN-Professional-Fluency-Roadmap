Curiosity & Warmth

The best conversations don't start with "What do you do?" They start with genuine curiosity about who someone is — and the warmth to make them feel safe sharing. Today you'll learn to open conversations that create real connection.

Curiosity & Warmth

Today's Philosophy

From Transaction to Connection

Most people start conversations transactionally: "What do you do? Where are you from? Nice weather." But C1 connectors start with genuine curiosity: "What got you interested in that field? What's been the most rewarding part?" The difference? Surface-level vs. soul-level. Small talk vs. real talk.

Why most conversations stay shallow
"Hi, I'm [name]. What do you do? Oh, interesting. How long have you been doing that? Nice. Well, here's my card."
  • Nghe như interview, không phải conversation
  • Câu hỏi đóng (yes/no, one-word answers)
  • Không có emotional depth
  • Không có personal insight
  • Không tạo không gian để đối phương share
  • Kết thúc bằng transaction (business card) thay vì connection
What C1 connectors do differently
"I've been curious about something — what got you interested in sustainable design in the first place? Was there a moment that sparked it for you? What's been the most rewarding part of that journey? You know, most people don't ask me that — they just ask what I do. Thank you for asking something real."
  • "I've been curious about..." - Show genuine interest, not politeness
  • "What got you interested..." - Open-ended, invites story
  • "Was there a moment..." - Specific, invites memory
  • "Most rewarding part" - Focus on meaning, not facts
  • "Most people don't ask me that" - Meta-acknowledgment of depth
  • Creates space for vulnerability and real sharing

5 Curiosity & Warmth Patterns

5 patterns để bắt đầu conversations với sự tò mò chân thành và warmth. Click 🔊 để nghe phát âm.

1
"What got you interested in [field]?"
/wʌt ɡɒt juː ˈɪntrəstɪd ɪn [fiːld]/
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
Casual → Semi-formal
Hỏi về origin story thay vì current status. Invite storytelling, not facts. Khiến người ta nói về passion thay vì position.
Why C1?

"What got you interested" = invite origin story. Focus on passion, not position. When people talk about what they love, they light up. Natural way to bypass small talk and access what matters to them.

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "What got you interested in photography? Was there a moment that sparked it?"
🏠 Life "What got you interested in cooking Vietnamese food? Did someone teach you?"
💼 Business "What got you interested in sustainable design in the first place?"
💼 Business "What got you interested in fintech? Was there a specific moment?"
💡 Usage Notes: Dùng khi muốn hiểu motivation/passion. Tránh "What do you do?" (too transactional). Follow up với "Was there a moment..." để invite story.
2
"What's been the most rewarding part?"
/wʌts biːn ðə moʊst rɪˈwɔːrdŋ pɑːrt/
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
Casual → Semi-formal
Focus on meaning, not achievement. Invite reflection on what matters. Khiến người ta share về giá trị thay vì thành tích.
Why C1?

"Most rewarding" = focus on meaning, not metrics. "Has been" = invite reflection over time. When people reflect on what matters, conversations deepen naturally. Shows you care about their inner world.

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "What's been the most rewarding part of learning guitar?"
🏠 Life "What's been the most rewarding part of traveling solo?"
Business "What's been the most rewarding part of building your team?"
💼 Business "What's been the most rewarding part of this project?"
💡 Usage Notes: Dùng sau khi biết context. Invite reflection, not reporting. Creates emotional depth. Natural follow-up to "What got you interested..."
3
"What's something people misunderstand about [topic]?"
/wʌts ˈsʌmθɪŋ ˌpiːpəl ˌmɪsʌndərˈstænd əˈbaʊt [ˈtpɪk]/
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
Casual → Semi-formal
Invite insider insight. Make person feel seen as expert. Khiến người ta share wisdom thay vì facts.
Why C1?

"People misunderstand" = acknowledge their expertise. Invite them to share insider knowledge. Creates safe space for vulnerability and teaching. People love being seen as experts — it builds instant rapport.

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "What's something people misunderstand about being a parent?"
Life "What's something people misunderstand about living abroad?"
💼 Business "What's something people misunderstand about working in consulting?"
Business "What's something people misunderstand about startups?"
💡 Usage Notes: Dùng khi người kia có expertise. Tạo cơ hội để họ share wisdom. Build rapport qua respect. Powerful question for networking.
4
"If you had to start over, would you...?"
/ɪf juː hæd tuː stɑːrt ˈoʊvər, wʊd juː/
⭐⭐⭐⭐ High
Casual → Semi-formal
Hypothetical reflection. Invite deeper wisdom without pressure. Khiến người ta share lessons learned một cách thoải mái.
Why C1?

"If you had to start over" = hypothetical, low-pressure. Invite reflection on lessons learned. Create space for vulnerability and honesty. Natural way to access wisdom without making it feel like an interview.

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "If you had to start over with traveling, would you do anything differently?"
🏠 Life "If you had to start over learning English, what would you focus on?"
💼 Business "If you had to start over your career, would you take the same path?"
💼 Business "If you had to start over this project, what would you change?"
💡 Usage Notes: Dùng khi conversation đã có depth. Hypothetical = safe space. Invite wisdom sharing. Powerful for mentors and experienced people.
5
"What question do you wish more people asked?"
/wʌt ˈkwɛstʃən duː juː wɪʃ mɔːr ˈpiːpəl æskt/
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High
Semi-formal
Meta-question. Invite person to share what they care about most. Khiến người ta share điều thực sự quan trọng với họ.
Why C1?

"Wish more people asked" = meta-awareness. Invite them to share what's important to them. Create space for authenticity. Shows you care about their inner world, not just outer facts. Most powerful curiosity question.

Examples 🔊

🏠 Life "What question do you wish more people asked about traveling solo?"
🏠 Life "What question do you wish more people asked about being an artist?"
💼 Business "What question do you wish more people asked about your work?"
💼 Business "What question do you wish more people asked about leadership?"
💡 Usage Notes: Dùng khi muốn deep dive. Meta-question creates vulnerability. Powerful way to access what someone truly cares about. Best used after rapport is established.

Integrated Conversation Examples

Xem cách 5 curiosity patterns được kết hợp trong 2 conversations hoàn chỉnh. Click 🔊 để nghe toàn bộ conversation.

🏠 Conversation 1: The Dinner Party Connection

"You know, I've been curious about something. What got you interested in architecture? Was there a moment that sparked it for you?

[They share their origin story — a childhood memory of their grandfather's house.]

That's beautiful. What's been the most rewarding part of that journey? Like, when you look back at all the buildings you've designed, what stands out?

[They reflect on a community center that changed a neighborhood.]

Wow. What's something people misunderstand about architecture that you wish they knew?

[They share that it's not about buildings — it's about people and how spaces shape lives.]

That really resonates with me. If you had to start over, would you do anything differently?

[They pause, then share honestly about work-life balance struggles.]

Thank you for sharing that. You know, what question do you wish more people asked about your work? Because I feel like I've just scratched the surface.

[They smile and share something deeply personal about why they do what they do.]

That conversation lasted 45 minutes. We exchanged numbers. Six months later, they invited me to their firm's opening. All because I asked real questions instead of 'What do you do?'"

Analysis:

  • "What got you interested..." - Opens origin story
  • "Most rewarding part" - Invites reflection
  • "People misunderstand" - Acknowledges expertise
  • "If you had to start over" - Creates vulnerability
  • "What question do you wish..." - Meta-question, deepest level
  • Emotional arc: Surface curiosity → Story → Reflection → Vulnerability → Connection
  • Result: From stranger to real connection in 45 minutes

💼 Conversation 2: The Client Coffee Meeting

"So before we talk about the project, I'm curious — what got you interested in sustainable fashion in the first place? Was there a moment that sparked it?

[They share about seeing a documentary in college that changed everything.]

That's powerful. What's been the most rewarding part of building this brand over the past 5 years?

[They talk about watching their first employee grow from intern to design director.]

I love that. What's something people misunderstand about sustainable fashion that frustrates you?

[They share passionately about greenwashing and how hard real sustainability is.]

That's really insightful. If you had to start over, would you take the same path, or would you do anything differently?

[They pause, then share honest doubts about scaling vs. staying true to values.]

Thank you for being real with me. What question do you wish more people asked about your work? Because I want to understand what matters most to you.

[They share about wanting to change the industry, not just build a brand.]

They signed the contract that day. Not because of my pitch — but because I made them feel seen. Six months later, they referred me to three other clients."

Analysis:

  • Same 5 patterns, different context
  • Professional but still deeply personal
  • Result: Client signed + became referral source
  • Key insight: Curiosity > pitch. Connection > transaction.
  • Emotional arc: Polite → Curious → Passionate → Vulnerable → Trusted

Curiosity & Warmth Mastery

Success Criteria

Can make someone feel seen and heard in conversation
Can bypass small talk and access what matters
Can create emotional connection through curiosity
Can leave people feeling valued and understood
Can turn strangers into real connections

Pro Tips

Listen more than you ask: Curiosity without listening is interrogation. Let them finish before asking follow-up.
Follow the energy: If they light up about a topic, go deeper there. Don't force your agenda.
Be genuinely curious: If you wouldn't remember the answer, it's not a real question. Ask because you care.
Share something first: Vulnerability invites vulnerability. Share a bit about yourself before asking deep questions.
Thank them for sharing: Acknowledge when someone opens up. "Thank you for sharing that" creates safety for next time.
